🔵 PROJECT 15: "Better, Worse, the Same"

You're comparing restaurants, cities, people — how do you express more, less, equal, and the best?

🎯 THE HYPOTHETICAL PROBLEM

→ It's Sunday afternoon — you're walking through Lyon with two classmates, Sofia and Kenji
→ You've just finished lunch at a bistrot and you're discussing the meal
→ Sofia says: "C'était bon, mais le restaurant de la semaine dernière était meilleur." (It was good, but last week's restaurant was better.)
→ Kenji adds: "Oui, et c'était moins cher aussi." (Yes, and it was less expensive too.)
→ You want to join the conversation, but you're stuck:
→ → You can describe things: "The restaurant is good." (C'est bon.) ✅
→ → But you CANNOT compare: "This restaurant is better than that one." ❌
→ → You CANNOT rank: "This is the best restaurant in Lyon." ❌
→ → You CANNOT equalize: "This restaurant is as good as that one." ❌
→ Every conversation involves comparison — food, weather, cities, people, prices, experiences
→ Without comparisons, you can only describe things in isolation — never in relation to each other

The core realization:
→ English comparisons are chaotic:
→ → Some add "-er": taller, faster, cheaper
→ → Some use "more": more beautiful, more expensive, more interesting
→ → Some are irregular: good → better, bad → worse
→ French comparisons are MORE SYSTEMATIC — almost everything follows ONE pattern:
→ → Plus + adjective + que = more... than
→ → Moins + adjective + que = less... than
→ → Aussi + adjective + que = as... as
→ Only TWO adjectives are irregular: bon (good) and mauvais (bad)
→ Master the pattern + the two irregulars → you can compare ANYTHING

🔍 HOW TO APPROACH IT

→ French comparisons have three degrees:

DegreeWhat It DoesFrench ToolEnglish Equivalent
ComparatifCompares TWO thingsplus/moins/aussi...quemore/less/as...than/as
SuperlatifRanks ONE thing as the TOP or BOTTOMle/la/les plus/moinsthe most/the least
ÉgalitéSays two things are THE SAMEaussi...que / autant...queas...as / as much...as

→ These tools work with:
→ → Adjectives (tall, beautiful, expensive)
→ → Adverbs (quickly, often, well)
→ → Nouns (more money, less time, as many friends)
→ → Verbs (works more, eats less, sleeps as much)

🔧 HOW CAN WE SOLVE IT

Step 1 — Comparing with Adjectives (The Core Pattern)

MORE... THAN = PLUS + adjective + QUE

Subject + ÊTRE + PLUS + ADJECTIVE + QUE + comparison point

→ Paris est plus grand que Lyon. (Paris is bigger than Lyon.)
→ This film est plus intéressant que l'autre. (This film is more interesting than the other.)

LESS... THAN = MOINS + adjective + QUE

Subject + ÊTRE + MOINS + ADJECTIVE + QUE + comparison point

→ Lyon est moins grand que Paris. (Lyon is less big than Paris.)
→ Ce restaurant est moins cher que l'autre. (This restaurant is less expensive than the other.)

AS... AS = AUSSI + adjective + QUE

Subject + ÊTRE + AUSSI + ADJECTIVE + QUE + comparison point

→ Lyon est aussi beau que Paris. (Lyon is as beautiful as Paris.)
→ Ce restaurant est aussi bon que l'autre. (This restaurant is as good as the other.)

🧠 THE GOLDEN FORMULA:

Comparison TypeFormula
MorePLUS + adj + QUE
LessMOINS + adj + QUE
EqualAUSSI + adj + QUE

→ This works for EVERY adjective in French (except bon and mauvais — Step 3)
→ No "-er" endings like English — French ALWAYS uses plus/moins/aussi
→ → English: taller / more beautiful (two systems)
→ → French: plus grand / plus beau (one system — always "plus")

Step 2 — Adjective Agreement in Comparisons

→ The adjective still agrees in gender and number with the SUBJECT (recall Project 1)

SubjectExampleAgreement
Masculine singularIl est plus grand que moi.grand (masc sing)
Feminine singularElle est plus grande que moi.grande (fem sing)
Masculine pluralIls sont plus grands que nous.grands (masc plural)
Feminine pluralElles sont plus grandes que nous.grandes (fem plural)

→ Plus/moins/aussi NEVER change — they are invariable
→ Only the adjective changes for agreement
→ "Que" never changes either

Step 3 — The Two Irregulars: BON and MAUVAIS

BON (good) → MEILLEUR (better) → LE MEILLEUR (the best)

DegreeMasculine SingFeminine SingMasculine PluralFeminine Plural
Goodbonbonnebonsbonnes

→ ✅ Ce restaurant est meilleur que l'autre. (This restaurant is better than the other.)
→ ❌ Ce restaurant est plus bon que l'autre. (WRONG — never say "plus bon")
→ "Plus bon" does NOT exist — always use "meilleur"

MAUVAIS (bad) → PIRE (worse) → LE PIRE (the worst)

DegreeFormNotes
Badmauvais(e)Normal adjective
Worsepire OR plus mauvais(e)Both are acceptable

→ ✅ Ce film est pire que l'autre. (This film is worse than the other.)
→ ✅ Ce film est plus mauvais que l'autre. (Also correct — both work)
→ "Pire" is more common and more natural in speech
→ Unlike "bon" → "meilleur" (mandatory), "mauvais" → "pire" is preferred but "plus mauvais" is acceptable

Step 4 — The Superlative: THE MOST / THE LEAST / THE BEST

Formula:

Subject + ÊTRE + LE/LA/LES + PLUS/MOINS + ADJECTIVE + (DE + group)

→ C'est le plus grand restaurant de Lyon. (It's the biggest restaurant in Lyon.)
→ C'est la plus belle ville de France. (It's the most beautiful city in France.)
→ C'est le moins cher. (It's the least expensive.)

🧠 KEY DETAILS:

ElementRule
Le/La/LesMatches the gender/number of the noun being described
De= "in" or "of" for the group → "de Lyon" = "in Lyon" / "de la classe" = "in the class"
AdjectiveStill agrees in gender/number with the noun

Superlative of BON and MAUVAIS:

AdjectiveSuperlativeExample
Bonle/la meilleur(e)C'est le meilleur restaurant. (the best)
Mauvaisle/la pireC'est le pire film. (the worst)

→ NEVER say "le plus bon" → always "le meilleur"

Step 5 — Comparing with Adverbs (How You Do Things)

BIEN (well) → MIEUX (better) → LE MIEUX (the best)

DegreeAdverbExample
WellbienIl parle bien.
BettermieuxIl parle mieux que moi.
The bestle mieuxC'est lui qui parle le mieux.

→ ✅ Elle chante mieux que moi. (She sings better than me.)
→ ❌ Elle chante plus bien que moi. (WRONG — never say "plus bien")
→ "Plus bien" does NOT exist — always use "mieux"

🧠 MEILLEUR vs. MIEUX — The #1 confusion:

WordTypeModifiesExample
Meilleur(e)ADJECTIVEA NOUNCe restaurant est meilleur. (This restaurant is better.)
MieuxADVERBA VERBIl cuisine mieux. (He cooks better.)

→ Meilleur = describes a THING (adjective → agrees in gender/number)
→ Mieux = describes an ACTION (adverb → never changes form)
→ → "The food is better" = La nourriture est meilleure. (adjective — describes noun)
→ → "He cooks better" = Il cuisine mieux. (adverb — describes verb)

Regular adverb comparisons:

AdverbMoreLessAs

souvent (often)plus souvent quemoins souvent queaussi souvent que
tard (late)plus tard quemoins tard queaussi tard que

loin (far)plus loin quemoins loin queaussi loin que

→ Same formula: plus/moins/aussi + adverb + que
→ Regular adverbs follow the same pattern as adjectives — no irregulars (except bien → mieux)

Step 6 — Comparing with Nouns (More/Less/As Much OF Something)

Formula:

ComparisonStructureExample
More ofPLUS DE + noun + QUEIl a **plus d'**argent que moi. (He has more money than me.)
Less ofMOINS DE + noun + QUEJ'ai moins de temps que toi. (I have less time than you.)
As much ofAUTANT DE + noun + QUEElle a **autant d'**amis que moi. (She has as many friends as me.)

→ Notice: with nouns, equality uses AUTANT DE (not "aussi de")
→ Aussi = for adjectives and adverbs
→ Autant = for nouns and verbs

Step 7 — Comparing with Verbs (Do Something More/Less/As Much)

Formula:

ComparisonStructureExample
MoreVERB + PLUS QUEIl travaille plus que moi. (He works more than me.)
LessVERB + MOINS QUEJe dors moins que toi. (I sleep less than you.)
As muchVERB + AUTANT QUEElle mange autant que lui. (She eats as much as him.)

→ With verbs: plus/moins/autant go AFTER the verb
→ Same as frequency word placement (Project 8)
→ Autant (not aussi) for verbs — same rule as nouns

🧠 Sentence 20 — "QUE JE PENSAIS":
→ = "than I thought" — que can be followed by a full clause, not just a noun/pronoun
→ "Plus/moins/aussi...que + subject + verb" = comparison with a situation
→ → C'est plus facile que je pensais. (It's easier than I thought.)
→ → C'est moins loin que tu crois. (It's less far than you think.)
→ → Il fait plus froid qu'hier. (It's colder than yesterday.)
→ Que becomes qu' before a vowel: que + hier → **qu'**hier

🧠 FRENCH PREFERENCE: "MOINS" vs. English "NOT AS":
→ English often says "not as big" instead of "less big"
→ French uses "moins" naturally and frequently — it doesn't sound awkward like "less big" does in English
→ → "Lyon est moins grand que Paris" = completely natural French
→ → You can also say: "Lyon n'est pas aussi grand que Paris" (is not as big as) — also correct but longer

🧠 NEGATIVE EQUALITY — "NOT AS... AS":
→ To say something is NOT equal → negate "aussi...que":

Subject + N'EST PAS AUSSI + adjective + QUE + comparison

→ Lyon n'est pas aussi grand que Paris. (Lyon is not as big as Paris.)
→ This is an alternative to "moins...que" — same meaning, different structure
→ → "Lyon est moins grand que Paris" = "Lyon n'est pas aussi grand que Paris"
→ Both are correct — "moins" is more direct, "pas aussi" is softer

🧠 SUPERLATIVE STRUCTURE — TWO POSITIONS:

Position 1: Before the noun (short common adjectives)

LE/LA/LES + PLUS/MOINS + ADJECTIVE + NOUN + DE + group

→ C'est le plus grand musée de France.
→ C'est la meilleure boulangerie de la ville.

Position 2: After the noun (most adjectives)

LE/LA/LES + NOUN + LE/LA/LES + PLUS/MOINS + ADJECTIVE + DE + group

→ C'est le jour le plus long de l'année.
→ C'est la personne la plus gentille que je connaisse.

🧠 Which position?
→ Adjectives that normally go BEFORE the noun (Project 3: bon, grand, petit, beau, jeune, vieux...) → superlative goes BEFORE
→ Adjectives that normally go AFTER the noun (most adjectives) → superlative goes AFTER
→ When superlative goes AFTER → the article appears TWICE:
→ → "Le jour le plus long" — first "le" = the day, second "le" = the most
→ This double article is a key feature of post-noun superlatives

🧠 "DE" in superlatives = "in" or "of":
→ Le plus grand de France = the biggest in France
→ Le meilleur du monde = the best in the world (de + le = du — Project 7)
→ La plus belle de la classe = the most beautiful in the class

→ ❌ Do NOT say "plus bon" → always "meilleur" — this is the #1 comparison error

🚫 WHEN NOT TO USE

→ Do NOT use comparatives when there's only ONE thing — use superlatives or simple descriptions
→ Do NOT use "plus bon" or "plus bien" — EVER — these forms don't exist in correct French
→ Do NOT use superlatives with "que" → superlatives use "de" for the group
→ → "Le plus grand que la France" → "Le plus grand de France"
→ Do NOT use "moins bon" when "pire" or "moins bien" is more natural
→ → "Moins bon" is acceptable for food quality → "Ce gâteau est moins bon"
→ → But for general "worse" → use "pire"
→ Do NOT use comparisons in every sentence — French often uses simple adjectives + context
