🔵 PROJECT 10: "How Things Used to Be"

You're describing your childhood, old habits, and scenes from the past

🎯 THE HYPOTHETICAL PROBLEM

→ It's Wednesday evening at your host family's home in Lyon
→ After dinner, the family starts sharing memories — "Quand j'étais petit..." (When I was little...)
→ The grandmother talks about life in the 1960s — how things were, what people used to do, how the town looked
→ The father describes his childhood summers — what he would do every day, how the weather was, how he felt
→ They turn to you: "Et toi ? C'était comment ton enfance ?" (And you? What was your childhood like?)
→ You try to answer using passé composé (Project 9):
→ → "J'ai été petit. J'ai habité dans une grande maison. J'ai joué dehors."
→ This sounds completely wrong — like a list of one-time events
→ You're describing a state (being small), a situation (living in a house), a habit (playing outside)
→ These are NOT completed events — they're backgrounds, atmospheres, ongoing realities of the past
→ You need a DIFFERENT past tense — one that paints the scenery instead of listing the events

The core realization:
→ French has two main past tenses that work as a PAIR — like two layers of a painting:

LayerTenseFunctionAnalogy
BackgroundImparfaitThe scenery, atmosphere, feelings, habits, descriptionsThe canvas and sky of a painting
ForegroundPassé composé (Project 9)The events, actions, things that happenedThe figures and action in the painting

→ You CANNOT tell a proper story with only one of these tenses
→ Project 9 gave you the foreground (events)
→ Project 10 gives you the background (descriptions)
→ Project 11 will teach you how to layer them together in real narratives

🔍 HOW TO APPROACH IT

→ The imparfait is used for 5 specific functions in the past:

FunctionWhat It DescribesExample (English)
1. DescriptionHow things looked, felt, wereThe sky was blue. He was tall.
2. Ongoing stateEmotions, conditions, situationsI was tired. She was happy.
3. Habitual actionThings you used to do regularlyI used to play outside. We would eat at noon.
4. Background actionWhat was happening when something else occurredI was walking when...
5. Age / Time / WeatherSetting the sceneIt was 3 PM. He was 10 years old. It was raining.

→ Notice: NONE of these are "events" — they're all states, scenes, and patterns
→ If you can add "used to" or "was/were ...ing" in English → it's probably imparfait in French

🔧 HOW CAN WE SOLVE IT

Step 1 — Forming the Imparfait: ONE Rule for ALL Verbs

This is the EASIEST tense to conjugate in French — almost no exceptions

The formula:

Take the NOUS form of present tense → Remove -ONS → Add imparfait endings

The endings (same for ALL verbs):

SubjectEndingPronunciation
Je-ais"ay"
Tu-ais"ay"
Il / Elle / On-ait"ay"
Nous-ions"ee-on"
Vous-iez"ee-ay"
Ils / Elles-aient"ay"

🔊 CRITICAL pronunciation fact:
→ Je, tu, il, ils forms ALL sound identical → "ay"
→ Only nous (-ions) and vous (-iez) sound different
→ Same pattern as present tense -ER verbs (Project 2) — spoken French relies heavily on the subject to distinguish

🔀 WHEN TO USE WHAT

Imparfait vs. Passé Composé — The Decision Guide

Use IMPARFAIT When...Use PASSÉ COMPOSÉ When...
Describing how things wereTelling what happened
Describing a state (tired, happy, cold)An action started and finished
Something was ongoing / in progressSomething occurred at a specific moment
An action was habitual / repeatedAn action happened a specific number of times
Setting the scene (weather, time, atmosphere)Reporting a sequence of events
Describing what someone looked like / feltTelling what someone did
Background — the canvasForeground — the action

Verb-by-Verb Guide:

VerbImparfait MeaningPassé Composé Meaning
êtrewas (state) → J'étais fatigué.was (became, in one moment) → rare at this level
avoirhad (ongoing) → J'avais un chien.had (received) → J'ai eu un cadeau.
savoirknew (ongoing) → Je savais la vérité.found out (moment of discovery) → J'ai su la vérité.
connaîtreknew (familiarity) → Je connaissais Paris.met (first time) → J'ai connu Marie en 2020.
vouloirwanted (ongoing desire) → Je voulais partir.wanted (attempted/decided) → J'ai voulu partir.
pouvoircould (ongoing ability) → Je pouvais voir.managed to (succeeded) → J'ai pu entrer.
devoirhad to (ongoing obligation) → Je devais travailler.had to (and did it) → J'ai dû partir.

→ The imparfait version = the state existed
→ The passé composé version = something happened (a change, a result, a moment)

🚫 WHEN NOT TO USE

→ Do NOT use imparfait for sudden events → "Soudain, il a crié." (Suddenly, he screamed.) — passé composé
→ Do NOT use imparfait for sequences → "J'ai mangé, j'ai payé, je suis parti." — all passé composé
→ Do NOT use imparfait with "une fois" (once) → "Une fois, j'ai vu un ours." — specific event → PC
→ Do NOT use imparfait with "soudain / tout à coup" (suddenly) → these trigger passé composé
→ Do NOT use imparfait for things that happened a specific number of times → "J'ai visité Paris trois fois." — PC
→ Do NOT use imparfait alone to tell a story — you need passé composé for the EVENTS (Project 11)

🔑 KEY TAKEAWAY

Project 9 → You tell what HAPPENED (events, actions, completed moments) — Passé Composé
Project 10 → You describe how things WERE (states, scenes, habits, feelings) — Imparfait

The imparfait is the easiest tense to conjugate — one formula works for every verb except être:

Nous present form → remove -ons → add: -ais, -ais, -ait, -ions, -iez, -aient

Five functions of imparfait:

  1. Description — C'était beau. Les rues étaient vides.
  2. Ongoing state — J'étais fatigué. Elle avait peur.
  3. Habitual action — Je jouais dehors tous les jours.
  4. Background action — Les enfants jouaient quand...
  5. Scene-setting — Il faisait beau. Il était trois heures.

You now have BOTH past tenses — but you don't yet know how to USE THEM TOGETHER.
→ Real French stories are a constant dance between PC and imparfait
→ "I was walking (imparfait) when I saw (PC) a friend."
→ "It was raining (imparfait) so we decided (PC) to go to a café."
→ This layering technique is what makes French storytelling come alive.
