🟡 PROJECT 25: "He Said That, She Told Me"

You're reporting what someone else said to a third person — and the tenses shift beneath you

🎯 THE HYPOTHETICAL PROBLEM

→ You're at work in France — your boss tells you something in the morning:

BOSS (at 9am): "La réunion est à 15h. J'ai déjà envoyé l'email. Nous partirons à 14h30."

→ At lunch, your colleague asks: "Qu'est-ce que le chef a dit ?"
→ You need to REPORT what the boss said — but you can't just repeat his exact words
→ You try:

❌ "Il a dit: la réunion est à 15h, j'ai déjà envoyé l'email, nous partirons à 14h30."

→ This sounds like you're quoting — it works in writing with quotation marks
→ But in spoken French, people report speech INDIRECTLY:

✅ "Il a dit que la réunion ÉTAIT à 15h."
✅ "Il a dit qu'il AVAIT déjà envoyé l'email."
✅ "Il a dit que nous PARTIRIONS à 14h30."

→ Notice: every tense shifted backward
→ "est" (présent) → "était" (imparfait)
→ "ai envoyé" (passé composé) → "avait envoyé" (plus-que-parfait)
→ "partirons" (futur simple) → "partirions" (conditionnel)

→ Your friend tells you about her weekend:

FRIEND: "Je suis allée à Lyon. C'était magnifique. J'y retournerai bientôt."

→ You tell another friend:

✅ "Elle a dit qu'elle ÉTAIT allée à Lyon."
✅ "Elle a dit que c'AVAIT ÉTÉ magnifique." (or "que c'était magnifique")
✅ "Elle a dit qu'elle Y RETOURNERAIT bientôt."

→ This is DISCOURS INDIRECT (reported speech / indirect speech)
→ It's one of the most used structures in daily conversation
→ Every time you say "Il a dit que...", "Elle m'a demandé si...", "Ils ont répondu que..." — you're using it
→ Without mastering the tense shift, your reported speech will be grammatically broken

The core realization:
→ When you report speech in the present ("Il dit que...") → NO tense shift needed
→ When you report speech in the past ("Il a dit que...") → ALL tenses shift backward
→ This shift is called la concordance des temps (tense agreement/sequence of tenses)
→ It follows a strict, predictable pattern — learn the pattern once, apply it forever

🔍 HOW TO APPROACH IT

→ Reported speech has three transformations that happen simultaneously:

TRANSFORMATION 1: REPORTING VERB + QUE
   Direct:    "Je suis fatigué."
   Indirect:  Il a dit QU'il était fatigué.

TRANSFORMATION 2: TENSE SHIFT (when reporting verb is past)
   "est" → "était"
   "ai fait" → "avait fait"
   "ferai" → "ferait"

TRANSFORMATION 3: PRONOUN / POSSESSIVE SHIFT
   "je" → "il/elle"
   "mon" → "son"
   "ici" → "là" / "là-bas"
   "aujourd'hui" → "ce jour-là"
   "demain" → "le lendemain"

→ Think of it as moving the camera — you're no longer IN the scene
→ You're reporting it from OUTSIDE — so perspective and time shift

🔧 HOW CAN WE SOLVE IT

DIRECT QUESTION:     "Tu viens ?"       (question form)

KEY RULES:
1. YES/NO questions → SI (never "que")
2. The reported question becomes a STATEMENT — no "?" no inversion no "est-ce que"
3. Same tense shift rules apply
4. SI contracts to S' before IL/ILS:
   → ...s'il faisait beau (NOT "si il")
   → ...s'ils avaient compris (NOT "si ils")
5. SI does NOT contract before ELLE/ELLES/ON:
   → ...si elle venait ✅ (NOT "s'elle")

Do NOT confuse:
→ SI = "if" (conditional — Project 18): Si il pleut, je resterai.
→ SI = "if/whether" (reported question — Project 25): Il a demandé si je venais.

Same word, different function — context makes it clear

🧠 Structure unlocked:

AFFIRMATIVE COMMAND → de + infinitif:
"Viens !"      → Il m'a dit DE VENIR.
"Attendez !"   → Il nous a demandé D'ATTENDRE.

NEGATIVE COMMAND → de ne pas + infinitif:
"Ne parle pas !" → Il m'a dit DE NE PAS PARLER.
"Ne t'inquiète pas !" → Il m'a dit DE NE PAS M'INQUIÉTER.

KEY RULES:
1. Impératif disappears entirely — replaced by DE + INFINITIF
2. Negative: DE + NE PAS + INFINITIF (ne pas stays together BEFORE infinitif)
3. Reflexive pronouns adjust to match the reported subject:
   → "Assieds-toi" (tu) → de M'asseoir (me — because I'm the one being told)
   → "Dépêche-toi" → de ME dépêcher
4. Object pronouns also adjust:
   → "Appelle-moi" (moi = her) → de L'appeler (her = elle)
