🟠 PROJECT 35: "I Should Have, I Could Have"

A day when everything went wrong — reflecting on what should, could, and would have been

🎯 THE HYPOTHETICAL PROBLEM

→ You had the worst day of your life in Paris
→ You woke up late → missed a job interview → forgot your friend's birthday → said the wrong thing to your partner → lost your wallet → got caught in the rain without an umbrella
→ Now you're sitting at home at 11 PM, replaying every mistake
→ Your brain keeps saying:

  • "I should have set an alarm"
  • "I could have called to reschedule"
  • "I would have liked to be there for her birthday"
  • "He shouldn't have reacted like that"
  • "We would have had a great evening if..."
    → In Project 34, you learned to express these regrets inside si-clauses — "If I had done X, Y would have happened"
    → But right now, you don't need conditions — you just need to express pure standalone regret, reproach, and missed possibility
    → You try: "Je dois appeler" — but that means "I must call" (NOW — present obligation from P6)
    → You try: "Je devrais appeler" — that means "I should call" (NOW — polite suggestion from P17)
    → Neither is right — you need: "J'aurais dû appeler" — "I should have called" (PAST — it's too late)

The core realization:
→ P34 taught conditionnel passé with a si-clause as its trigger
→ P35 teaches conditionnel passé standing alone — no condition, just emotion
→ Combined with modal verbs (devoir, pouvoir, vouloir, aimer, falloir), it becomes the full spectrum of human regret

🔍 HOW TO APPROACH IT

→ Think of the conditionnel passé as having two lives:

LifeWhere It LivesWhat It DoesProject
Life 1Inside si-clausesResult of an unreal past conditionP34
Life 2Standalone — no si-clauseExpresses regret, reproach, missed chance, polite past hypothesisP35 (NOW)

→ In Life 2, the conditionnel passé most often combines with modal verbs:

Modal VerbConditionnel Passé FormMeaning
Devoirj'aurais dû + infinitifI should have / I ought to have
Pouvoirj'aurais pu + infinitifI could have / I might have
Vouloirj'aurais voulu + infinitifI would have wanted to / I would have liked to
Aimerj'aurais aimé + infinitifI would have loved to
Falloiril aurait fallu + infinitifIt would have been necessary to / One should have
Savoirj'aurais su + infinitifI would have known how to

→ But conditionnel passé also works with any verb for standalone hypothetical past statements:

  • "J'aurais accepté." = I would have accepted.
  • "Elle serait venue." = She would have come.
  • "On aurait compris." = We would have understood.

✅ WHAT SHOULD DO

→ Memorize the "Big Three" modal combos first:

  1. Aurais dû = should have (regret about inaction / reproach)
  2. Aurais pu = could have (missed possibility / consolation)
  3. Aurais voulu / aimé = would have wanted/loved (gentle longing)

→ Practice negative forms equally:

  • N'aurais pas dû = shouldn't have (regret about action taken)
  • N'aurais pas pu = couldn't have (impossibility defense)
  • N'aurais jamais dû = should never have (intense regret — P39)

→ Recognize the journalism function (Block I) — completely separate from regret
→ Link to P6 — present modals → Link to P17 — conditionnel modals → P35 — conditionnel passé modals
→ Link to P34 — some sentences naturally attract si-clauses; let them come when they feel right
→ Use "Ça aurait pu être pire" immediately — it's a universal French comfort phrase

❌ WHAT SHOULD NOT DO

→ ❌ Do NOT confuse the three modal timelines:

❌ Wrong ConfusionWhat It Actually MeansWhat You Probably Meant
"Je dois appeler"I must call NOWIf you mean past: "J'aurais dû appeler"
"Je devrais appeler"I should call (now, advice)If you mean past: "J'aurais dû appeler"
"J'aurais dû appeler"I should have called (too late)✅ Correct for past regret

→ ❌ Do NOT use être auxiliary for avoir verbs in conditionnel passé:

  • ❌ Je serais dû appeler → devoir uses AVOIR → J'aurais dû appeler
  • ❌ Elle serait pu venir → pouvoir uses AVOIR → Elle aurait pu venir

→ ❌ Do NOT forget agreement with être verbs:

  • ❌ Elle serait venu → Elle serait venue
  • ❌ Elles seraient parti → Elles seraient parties

→ ❌ Do NOT confuse "aurais pu" (could have) with "aurais dû" (should have):

  • "J'aurais pu l'aider" = I had the ability but didn't → missed chance
  • "J'aurais dû l'aider" = I had the obligation but didn't → moral regret
  • Different emotional weight — different social meaning

→ ❌ Do NOT use conditionnel passé for things that DID happen:

  • ❌ "J'aurais mangé ce matin" (when you DID eat) → This means you DIDN'T eat
  • ✅ "J'ai mangé ce matin" = I ate this morning (passé composé — P9 — real past)

→ ❌ Do NOT mix up the journalism use with the regret use:

  • "Il aurait démissionné" in a NEWS context = He reportedly resigned (unverified fact)
  • "Il aurait démissionné" in a PERSONAL context = He would have resigned (hypothetical)
  • Context determines meaning — same form, different function

🚫 WHEN NOT TO USE

→ When the event ACTUALLY HAPPENED → use passé composé (P9) or plus-que-parfait (P32)

  • "I called him" = Je l'ai appelé ✅ (real past)
  • NOT: "Je l'aurais appelé" (this means I WOULD HAVE called — but didn't)

→ When giving present advice → use conditionnel présent (P17)

  • "You should call him" (now) = Tu devrais l'appeler
  • NOT: "Tu aurais dû l'appeler" (this is about the PAST — too late)

→ When stating a real obligation → use présent (P6)

  • "I must leave" = Je dois partir
  • NOT: "J'aurais dû partir" (this means I should have left already)

→ When building a si-clause with a condition → P34 already covers this

  • "If I had known, I would have..." = P34 territory
  • P35 = when there is NO si-clause — just standalone emotional expression

→ When expressing a polite current request → use conditionnel présent (P17)

  • "I would like a coffee" = Je voudrais un café (now)
  • NOT: "J'aurais voulu un café" (this means "I would have liked a coffee" — past, missed)
  • EXCEPTION: "J'aurais voulu vous poser une question" = very formal politeness — deliberately using past hypothetical for extreme softness → discussed in P48 (cultural calibration)

🔑 KEY TAKEAWAY

Project 34 gave you conditionnel passé INSIDE si-clauses — triggered by an unreal past condition.
Project 35 frees conditionnel passé to stand ALONE — expressing pure regret, reproach, longing, impossibility, and even unverified news.

The Big Three modal combos are the emotional backbone of French adult speech:

  • Aurais dû = the weight of missed obligation
  • Aurais pu = the sting of missed possibility (or the comfort of "it could have been worse")
  • Aurais voulu/aimé = the tenderness of unfulfilled desire

These structures separate a learner who describes WHAT HAPPENED from a speaker who reflects on WHAT COULD HAVE BEEN.
