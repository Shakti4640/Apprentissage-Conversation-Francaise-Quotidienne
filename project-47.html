🔴 PROJECT 47: "How They Actually Talk"

You're listening to a French podcast and can't understand a word — because textbook French and real French are two different languages

🎯 THE HYPOTHETICAL PROBLEM

→ You have completed 46 projects
→ You understand French grammar, tenses, conditionals, subjunctive, idioms, argumentation
→ You sit in a Parisian café
→ Two French people are talking next to you
→ You understand maybe 30%
→ Not because you lack vocabulary — but because what they say sounds NOTHING like what you learned
→ They drop sounds, merge words, skip entire grammatical particles, reshape sentences
→ You turn on a French podcast — same problem
→ You watch a French film — same problem
→ You read the subtitles and think: "THAT is what they said?!"

The devastating realization:
→ Everything from Projects 1–46 was written French mapped onto speech
→ Real spoken French is a compressed, reshaped, accelerated version of that written system
→ If you don't learn the compression rules — you will read French perfectly but understand spoken French poorly
→ This project bridges that gap — showing you every major transformation between written and spoken French

🔍 HOW TO APPROACH IT

→ Think of written French as HD uncompressed audio
→ Think of spoken French as MP3 — compressed, some frequencies removed, but the message survives
→ The compression follows predictable patterns
→ Once you learn the patterns — you can decompress what you hear back into structures you already know

The 10 Compression Rules of Spoken French:

Rule #Compression TypeWritten → Spoken
1"Ne" drops in negationJe ne sais pas → Je sais pas
2"Tu" contractsTu as → T'as / Tu es → T'es
3"Je" contractsJe suis → Chuis / Je sais → Chais
4"Il" contracts or dropsIl y a → Y'a / Il faut → Faut
5"Nous" disappearsNous allons → On va
6Questions flattenEst-ce que tu viens ? → Tu viens ?
7"Cela" becomes "ça" alwaysCela me plaît → Ça me plaît
8Syllables get eatenJe ne sais pas → Chais pas
9Filler words appear everywhere(silence) → Euh, bon, ben, enfin, quoi
10Formal words swap for casual onesNous avons → On a / Il y a → Y'a

→ For each compression rule — see the written version (what you learned) vs. the spoken version (what you'll actually hear)
→ Every sentence references the PROJECT where the written structure was originally taught
→ You already KNOW the grammar — now you learn how it sounds when French people actually use it

🔹 BLOCK A — COMPRESSION RULE 1: "Ne" Drops in Negation

(Reference: Project 5 — "Saying No in 10 Ways" + Project 39 — Advanced Negation)

The rule:
→ In written French: ne...pas / ne...jamais / ne...rien / ne...plus
→ In spoken French: "ne" is dropped 95% of the time
→ This is THE most universal spoken French rule — even educated speakers drop "ne"

#Written (What You Learned)Spoken (What You'll Hear)Phonetic Hint
1Je ne sais pas.Je sais pas."zhuh say pah"
2Je ne comprends pas.Je comprends pas."zhuh com-pron pah"
3Ce **n'**est pas vrai.C'est pas vrai."say pah vray"
4Il ne veut pas venir.Il veut pas venir."eel vuh pah vuh-neer"
5Elle **n'**a pas le temps.Elle a pas le temps."el ah pah luh ton"
6Je ne mange jamais ici.Je mange jamais ici."zhuh monzh zhah-may ee-see"
7Il **n'**y a plus de pain.Y'a plus de pain."yah plew duh pan"
8Je ne vois rien.Je vois rien."zhuh vwah ree-en"
9Il **n'**y a personne.Y'a personne."yah pair-son"
10Tu ne fais que dormir.Tu fais que dormir."tew fay kuh dor-meer"

🧠 Decompression skill: When you hear a sentence with pas / jamais / rien / plus / personne but NO "ne" — your brain should automatically insert the "ne" to match the structure you already know from Projects 5 and 39

🔹 BLOCK C — COMPRESSION RULE 3: "Je" Contracts and Reshapes

(Reference: Projects 1, 2, 6 — every project using "je")

The rule:
→ "Je" before a consonant → often sounds like "zh" (the "e" gets swallowed)
→ "Je suis" → "chuis" (the most iconic spoken French contraction)
→ "Je sais" → "chais"
→ "Je ne sais pas" → "chais pas" (three compressions at once!)

#WrittenSpokenPhonetic Hint
21Je suis fatigué.Chuis fatigué."shwee fah-tee-gay"
22Je suis là.Chuis là."shwee lah"
23Je sais.Chais."shay"
24Je ne sais pas.Chais pas."shay pah"
25Je suis en retard.Chuis en retard."shwee zon ruh-tar"
26Je pense que oui.J'pense que oui."zhpons kuh wee"
27Je crois que non.J'crois que non."zhkrwah kuh non"
28Je vais y aller.J'vais y aller."zhvay ee ah-lay"
29Je te dis la vérité.J'te dis la vérité."zhtuh dee lah vay-ree-tay"
30Je me demande pourquoi.J'me demande pourquoi."zhmuh duh-mond poor-kwah"

🧠 Decompression skill: When you hear "shwee" → that's "je suis" / When you hear "shay pah" → that's "je ne sais pas" — three words compressed into two syllables

🔹 BLOCK D — COMPRESSION RULE 4: "Il" Contracts or Disappears

(Reference: Project 3 — "Il y a" / Project 26 — "Il faut" / Project 27 — Impersonal structures)

The rule:
→ "Il y a" → "y'a" (the "il" vanishes completely)
→ "Il faut" → "faut" (the "il" vanishes)
→ "Il fait" → "fait" (in weather expressions)
→ "Il" before consonant → sometimes just "i"

🧠 Critical insight: "Y'a" is probably the single most frequent sound in spoken French — you will hear it hundreds of times per day — it replaces "il y a" from Project 3 in virtually all spoken contexts

🔹 BLOCK E — COMPRESSION RULE 5: "Nous" Disappears Entirely

(Reference: Project 1 — Subject pronouns / Every project using "nous")

The rule:
→ "Nous" is essentially dead in spoken French
→ 99% of spoken French uses "on" instead of "nous"
→ Verb conjugation shifts: "nous avons" → "on a" / "nous sommes" → "on est"
→ "Nous" only survives in very formal speeches, legal documents, academic writing

#Written / FormalSpoken / RealPhonetic Hint

42Nous avons mangé.On a mangé."on ah mon-zhay"

44Nous devons partir.On doit partir."on dwah par-teer"
45Nous nous sommes trompés.On s'est trompés."on say tron-pay"
46Nous ne savions pas.On savait pas."on sah-vay pah"
47Nous aurions aimé venir.On aurait aimé venir."on oh-ray ay-may vuh-neer"
48Nous pourrions essayer.On pourrait essayer."on poo-ray eh-say-yay"
49Est-ce que nous y allons ?On y va ?"on ee vah"
50Nous nous en occuperons.On s'en occupera."on son oh-kew-prah"

🧠 Reality check: If you use "nous" in casual conversation — French people will understand you but you'll sound like you're reading from a textbook. Always default to "on" in speech.

→ Also notice: "cinéma" becomes "ciné" — word truncation is another spoken pattern (see Block I)

🔹 BLOCK F — COMPRESSION RULE 6: Questions Flatten

(Reference: Project 4 — "Asking Without Sounding Lost")

The rule:
→ Project 4 taught three question forms: intonation / est-ce que / inversion
→ In spoken French: intonation dominates 80%+
→ "Est-ce que" → often shortened to just rising tone
→ Inversion → almost never used in casual speech
→ Question words often move to the END of the sentence (completely opposite of textbook)

#Written / TextbookSpoken / RealPhonetic Hint
51Qu'est-ce que tu fais ?Tu fais quoi ?"tew fay kwah"
52Où est-ce que tu vas ?Tu vas où ?"tew vah oo"
53Quand est-ce que tu arrives ?T'arrives quand ?"tah-reev kon"
54Comment est-ce que tu t'appelles ?Tu t'appelles comment ?"tew tah-pel koh-mon"
55Pourquoi est-ce que tu ris ?Tu ris pourquoi ? / Pourquoi tu ris ?"tew ree poor-kwah"
56Qu'est-ce que c'est ?C'est quoi ?"say kwah"
57Qu'est-ce qui se passe ?I'se passe quoi ?"ee spas kwah"
58Est-ce que tu viens ?Tu viens ?"tew vee-en" (just rising tone)
59À quelle heure est-ce qu'on part ?On part à quelle heure ?"on par ah kel ur"
60Avec qui est-ce que tu y vas ?Tu y vas avec qui ?"tew ee vah ah-vek kee"

🧠 The spoken French question formula:

TEXTBOOK:  Question Word + est-ce que + Subject + Verb ?
SPOKEN:    Subject + Verb + Question Word ? (with rising intonation)

→ This is a complete structural reversal — and it's how 80% of real questions are asked

🔹 BLOCK H — COMPRESSION RULE 8: Multi-Layer Compression (Stacked Rules)

(Reference: ALL previous projects — this is where everything compresses simultaneously)

The rule:
→ Real spoken French doesn't apply ONE compression at a time
→ It stacks 2, 3, or even 4 compressions in a single sentence
→ This is why beginners can't understand — multiple transformations happen at once

#Full Written FrenchEvery Compression AppliedRules Stacked

73Tu ne vas pas y aller ?Tu vas pas y aller ?Rule 1 + Rule 2 (intonation question)
74Je ne me souviens plus.J'me souviens plus.Rule 1 + Rule 3 (je contracts)
75Nous ne sommes pas encore arrivés.On est pas encore arrivés.Rule 1 + Rule 5 (nous→on)
76Qu'est-ce que tu en penses ?T'en penses quoi ?Rule 2 (tu→t') + Rule 6 (question flattens)
77Il n'y a personne qui est venu.Y'a personne qui est venu.Rule 1 + Rule 4
78Je ne suis pas en train de rigoler.Chuis pas en train de rigoler.Rule 1 + Rule 3
79Est-ce que tu ne voudrais pas essayer ?Tu voudrais pas essayer ?Rule 1 + Rule 6

🧠 Decompression exercise: When you hear a sentence you don't understand →

Step 1: Insert the "ne" back
Step 2: Expand "t'" back to "tu"
Step 3: Expand "chuis/chais" back to "je suis / je sais"
Step 4: Expand "y'a" back to "il y a"
Step 5: Replace "on" with "nous" mentally
Step 6: Move the question word back to the front
Step 7: Replace casual vocabulary with formal equivalents

→ Now you have the written French you already know from Projects 1–46

❌ WHAT SHOULD NOT DO

→ ❌ Do NOT speak textbook French in casual settings — you'll sound robotic

→ ❌ Do NOT try to learn spoken French BEFORE written French — you need the foundation (Projects 1–46) to decompress
→ ❌ Do NOT use ALL compressions when speaking to a boss, professor, or in formal settings — register matters (Project 40)
