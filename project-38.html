🟠 PROJECT 38: "While Doing, By Doing"

A day of multitasking — doing two things at once, describing how you accomplish tasks

🎯 THE HYPOTHETICAL PROBLEM

→ You are a freelance journalist in Paris — your days are a whirlwind of simultaneous actions
→ You eat breakfast while reading the news
→ You make phone calls while walking to the metro
→ You take notes while listening to an interview
→ You improve your French by practicing every day
→ You found your apartment by asking friends
→ You hurt your back while lifting a heavy box
→ In English, all of these use "-ing" — "while reading," "by practicing," "while lifting"
→ But in French, the "-ing" form works COMPLETELY differently
→ You try: "Je mange en lisant le journal" — ✅ correct!
→ You try: "Je suis mangeant" — ❌ completely wrong (French doesn't use -ing for continuous tenses)
→ You try: "En travaillant dur, j'ai réussi" — ✅ correct!
→ You try: "J'aime lisant" — ❌ wrong (French doesn't use -ing after verbs like English)
→ The gérondif is powerful but NARROW — it only works in specific contexts

The core realization:
→ English uses "-ing" for EVERYTHING: continuous tenses, gerunds, present participles, after prepositions
→ French uses "en + participe présent" (gérondif) for ONLY specific situations:

  1. Simultaneity — while doing
  2. Method/Means — by doing
  3. Manner — doing (how)
  4. Condition — if doing / provided that doing
  5. Cause — because of doing
    → It NEVER replaces a conjugated verb for ongoing actions ("I am eating" ≠ "Je suis mangeant")
    → Mastering the gérondif means knowing when to use it AND when NOT to use it

🔍 HOW TO APPROACH IT

→ Think of the gérondif as a verbal adverb — it modifies HOW, WHEN, or WHY the main action happens
→ It always refers to the same subject as the main verb — both actions done by the SAME person

MAIN ACTION ← modified by ← GÉRONDIF (en + participe présent)

→ "Je mange en lisant" = I eat (main) + while reading (modifier) → same person does both
→ ❌ "Je mange en Marie lisant" → IMPOSSIBLE — gérondif cannot have a different subject

🔧 HOW CAN WE SOLVE IT

The Five Meanings of the Gérondif

MeaningEnglish EquivalentQuestion AnsweredExample
Simultaneitywhile doingWHEN?Je mange en lisant (I eat while reading)
Method/Meansby doingHOW?J'ai réussi en travaillant (I succeeded by working)
Mannerdoing / -ingIN WHAT WAY?Il est parti en courant (He left running)
Conditionif doing / by doingUNDER WHAT CONDITION?En faisant attention, tu éviteras les erreurs (By being careful, you'll avoid mistakes)
Causebecause of doingWHY?En arrivant en retard, il a raté le début (By arriving late, he missed the beginning)

→ Context determines which meaning applies — the structure is identical for all five
→ The gérondif can go at the beginning or end of the sentence

Position in the Sentence

POSITION A (end — most common):
    Je travaille en écoutant de la musique.

POSITION B (beginning — emphasis on the gérondif):
    En écoutant de la musique, je travaille mieux.

→ When the gérondif starts the sentence → comma separates it from the main clause
→ When it ends the sentence → no comma needed

TOUT EN + Participe Présent — Emphasis on Simultaneity or Contrast

TOUT EN + participe présent = WHILE (emphasis) / EVEN WHILE / DESPITE doing

→ "Il sourit tout en sachant la vérité" = He smiles even while knowing the truth
→ Adds emphasis — stresses that BOTH actions happen at the SAME time
→ Can introduce a contrast — doing X even though Y is also happening

⚠️ Sentence 83 — The Same-Subject Rule

❌ Je l'ai vue en traversant la rue.
   Subject: JE    Gérondif subject: also JE
   = I saw her WHILE I WAS crossing the street (I crossed, not her)

✅ Je l'ai vue traversant la rue.  (participe présent — no en)
   OR: Je l'ai vue qui traversait la rue.
   = I saw her crossing the street (SHE was crossing)

→ The gérondif ALWAYS refers to the main subject
→ If you use "en traversant" → it means YOU were crossing, not her
→ To describe what SHE was doing → use participe présent (no en) or "qui + imparfait"
→ This is the #1 error English speakers make with the gérondif

  • French has NO "-ing" continuous tense

→ ❌ NEVER use gérondif after preference/opinion verbs:

  • ❌ J'aime en lisant → ✅ J'aime lire (infinitif)
  • ❌ Je déteste en attendant → ✅ Je déteste attendre

→ ❌ NEVER use gérondif as the subject of a sentence:

  • ❌ En nageant est bon → ✅ Nager est bon / La natation est bonne

→ ❌ NEVER use gérondif when subjects are DIFFERENT:

  • ❌ Je l'ai vu en traversant (meaning HE crossed) → ✅ Je l'ai vu qui traversait
  • If YOU crossed → then "en traversant" is correct

→ ❌ NEVER use gérondif for "before doing" or "after doing":

  • ❌ En manger avant → ✅ Avant de manger
  • ❌ En manger après → ✅ Après avoir mangé

→ ❌ NEVER use gérondif for "without doing":

  • ❌ en ne partant pas (meaning "without leaving") → ✅ Sans partir
  • Negative gérondif means "by NOT doing" — different from "without doing"

→ ❌ NEVER separate EN from the participe présent with non-reflexive pronouns:

  • ❌ En le faisant → ⚠️ Actually this IS acceptable in some contexts
  • Object pronouns of the infinitive embedded in gérondif CAN appear between en and -ant
  • But reflexive pronouns ALWAYS go there: en me levant, en se préparant

🔑 KEY TAKEAWAY

P37 gave you faire + infinitif — causing others to perform actions.
P38 gives you en + participe présent — connecting two actions performed by the SAME person.

The gérondif is one of the most elegant and efficient structures in French:

  • It replaces entire subordinate clauses: "pendant que je lisais" → "en lisant"
  • It has FIVE distinct meanings — all in one tiny structure
  • It has ONE absolute rule: same subject for both actions

But it is NOT the French equivalent of English "-ing":

  • French has NO continuous tense → "I am eating" ≠ "Je suis en mangeant"
  • After preference verbs → infinitif, not gérondif
  • Before/after/without → preposition + infinitif, not gérondif

The proverb says it all:
"C'est en forgeant qu'on devient forgeron."
It's by DOING that you become a DOER.

You now have three powerful verbal tools:

  • P36: Double pronouns → compressing objects
  • P37: Faire causatif → delegating actions
  • P38: Gérondif → connecting simultaneous actions
